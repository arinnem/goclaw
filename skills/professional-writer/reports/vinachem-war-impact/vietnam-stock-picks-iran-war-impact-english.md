## Executive summary

The US–Israel military strikes on Iran (beginning February 28, 2026) have triggered a supply shock across energy and commodity markets. Oil jumped 13% to $82/bbl, European gas surged 41%, and the Strait of Hormuz — through which ~20% of global oil and ~33% of global nitrogen fertilizer exports flow — has effectively shut down[^1][^2][^3].

For Vietnam's stock market, this creates a clear **sector rotation opportunity**. The playbook is straightforward:

- **Go long** on upstream oil & gas, fertilizer/chemicals, and port logistics — sectors with direct earnings uplift from commodity price increases.
- **Avoid or underweight** airlines, rubber/tire exporters to the US, and import-heavy consumer goods — sectors facing cost pressure with limited pricing power.
- **Consider gold exposure** as a geopolitical hedge — gold hit $5,408/oz globally, SJC gold in Vietnam surpassed VND 190 million/tael[^4][^5].

The thesis is time-sensitive. If the conflict resolves within 4–7 days (ING's Scenario 1), commodity prices normalize and this is a short trade. If it drags beyond 4 weeks (Scenario 2), earnings upgrades for beneficiary sectors become structural[^3].

## Methodology

### Screening framework

This note uses a 4-step sector screening methodology:

1. **Transmission channel mapping.** For each sector, identify whether the Iran conflict transmits through (a) input costs, (b) output prices, (c) supply chain disruption, or (d) demand shifts.
2. **Direction of impact.** Classify each transmission channel as positive (tailwind) or negative (headwind) for earnings.
3. **Magnitude assessment.** Estimate the significance of the impact using available commodity price data, analyst estimates, and historical precedent (2022 Russia–Ukraine war).
4. **Catalyst timing.** Assess whether the impact is immediate (trading opportunity) or delayed (earnings revision play).

### Scenario framework

All assessments are made under two scenarios from ING Economics[^3]:

| Scenario | Duration | Oil (Brent) | Gas (TTF) | Market implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 — Short war | 4–7 days | $77–82/bbl (fades) | Normalizes | Short-term trade, temporary premium |
| S2 — Prolonged | 4+ weeks | $100–140/bbl | €80–100/MWh | Structural earnings revision, inflation risk |

## Sector 1 — Oil & gas (STRONG OVERWEIGHT)

### Thesis

Vietnam's oil & gas sector is the most direct beneficiary. Higher Brent crude prices flow directly into (a) PetroVietnam's upstream revenues, (b) rig day-rates for drilling companies, (c) service contract values, and (d) refining margins (to a point).

### Key data points

- Brent crude rose 13% to $82/bbl on March 2; could reach $100–140 in S2[^1][^3]
- OPEC+ announced a modest 206,000 bpd output increase for April, but most of it must transit through the now-blocked Hormuz[^1]
- Iran pumps 4.5% of global supply; its own exports are directly disrupted[^1]
- Vietnam is a net oil producer (Petrovietnam), so higher prices are a direct revenue boost

### Ticker picks

| Ticker | Company | Market cap | Why it benefits | Catalyst |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GAS | PV Gas | Large | Largest gas distributor in Vietnam; higher gas prices directly improve margins | Immediate — gas prices already up |
| PVD | PV Drilling | Mid | Rig day-rates and utilization increase when oil is above $70; exploration budgets rise | Near-term — new contracts priced higher |
| PVS | PV Technical Services | Mid | Diversified services (M&C, marine); strong project backlog; less volatile than PVD | Medium-term — backlog execution |
| BSR | Binh Son Refining | Large | Vietnam's largest refiner; crack spreads tend to widen during supply shocks | Immediate — refining margin expansion |
| PLX | Petrolimex | Large | Downstream distribution; passes through cost increases; stock surges with oil | Immediate — sentiment driven |
| OIL | PV Oil | Mid | Crude trading company; higher oil = higher revenue | Immediate |
| PVT | PV Trans | Mid | Oil tanker operator; shipping rates increase during supply disruptions | Immediate — freight rates up |

**Risk to thesis:** If the conflict resolves quickly (S1), these names give back gains within 1–2 weeks. PLX and BSR are more sensitive to inventory valuation effects — positive when oil rises, negative when it falls.

### Historical precedent

During the June 2025 US–Israel strikes on Iran (a shorter episode), oil spiked briefly and then faded. GAS, PVD, and PVS rallied 5–10% and then pulled back. The current episode is more severe (Hormuz effectively closed), suggesting a larger and more sustained move[^6][^7].

## Sector 2 — Fertilizer & chemicals (OVERWEIGHT)

### Thesis

This is the highest-conviction sector. Three tailwinds converge simultaneously:

1. **Global urea prices surging.** Urea FOB New Orleans jumped ~$77 to $606/ton; FOB Egypt up 13% to $550/ton. The Middle East accounts for ~33% of global nitrogen fertilizer exports, nearly all transiting Hormuz[^8][^9].
2. **Import substitution.** Vietnamese producers (DPM, DCM) use domestic gas, not imported LNG, as feedstock. Their cost structure is partially insulated while global competitors face soaring input costs.
3. **Seasonal demand.** Northern hemisphere spring planting is underway — farmers need fertilizer now and cannot delay purchases[^8].

### Key data points

- Iran alone accounts for ~10% of global urea exports[^8]
- ~1/3 of global fertilizer trade passes through Hormuz[^8]
- China has maintained phosphate export restrictions, tightening supply further[^10]
- DGC is Asia's largest yellow phosphorus (P4) exporter; benefits from both phosphate scarcity and semiconductor demand[^10]

### Ticker picks

| Ticker | Company | Market cap | Why it benefits | Catalyst |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DPM | Phu My Fertilizer | Large | Vietnam's largest urea producer; domestic gas feedstock; direct price uplift from global urea rally | Immediate — urea prices already +14% |
| DCM | Ca Mau Fertilizer | Large | Second-largest urea producer; cost control strategy; diversified product mix; strong 2026 outlook from analysts[^11] | Immediate to medium-term |
| DGC | Duc Giang Chemicals | Large | Asia's #1 P4 exporter; self-sufficient in apatite; benefits from phosphorus scarcity + China export curbs + new Nghi Son plant in Q1/2026[^10] | Immediate + structural |
| BFC | Binh Dien Fertilizer | Mid | Mixed fertilizer (NPK); benefits from higher fertilizer prices generally | Medium-term — earnings revision |
| LAS | Lam Thao Superphosphate | Small | Domestic superphosphate producer; import substitution play | Medium-term |

**Risk to thesis:** If oil resolves quickly (S1), urea prices give back gains. However, the seasonal demand (spring planting) provides a floor that other commodities don't have. DGC has structural advantages beyond the war trade (semiconductor phosphorus demand, China export curbs).

**Top pick in this sector: DGC.** Multiple independent tailwinds (war premium + China restrictions + semiconductor cycle + new capacity), self-sufficient in raw materials, and a proven growth track record.

## Sector 3 — Port logistics & shipping (MODERATE OVERWEIGHT)

### Thesis

Supply chain disruption increases demand for port capacity and shipping services. Rerouting around Hormuz and potential Suez disruption (Maersk has halted both routes)[^1] extends voyage times and tightens vessel supply. Vietnamese ports benefit from increased transshipment demand.

### Ticker picks

| Ticker | Company | Why it benefits |
|---|---|---|
| GMD | Gemadept | Vietnam's largest private port operator; higher throughput if trade reroutes through Asia |
| HAH | Hai An Transport | Container shipping; freight rate uplift from global disruption |
| PVT | PV Trans | Oil tanker operator (also listed under oil & gas); double catalyst |

**Risk:** This is a secondary beneficiary — the impact is less direct than oil or fertilizer. Trade volumes could actually decline if global slowdown materializes (S2).

## Sector 4 — Gold / safe havens (TACTICAL OVERWEIGHT)

### Thesis

Gold is the classic geopolitical hedge. It rose 2.5% to $5,408/oz on March 2[^1]. In Vietnam, SJC gold bars exceeded VND 190 million/tael with a premium of ~VND 21.7 million over international prices[^5].

### Exposure options

There are no pure gold mining stocks on HOSE/HNX. Exposure can be gained through:
- **Physical gold** (SJC bars, 9999 gold rings)
- **Gold-adjacent ETFs** if available on Vietnamese platforms
- **PNJ (Phu Nhuan Jewelry):** While not a gold play per se, PNJ benefits from increased gold trading volumes and consumer interest in gold during crises. However, PNJ is primarily a retail jewelry business, so the link is indirect.

**Risk:** Gold already has a significant war premium. If the conflict resolves (S1), gold gives back gains quickly.

## Sectors to AVOID or UNDERWEIGHT

### Airlines (HVN, VJC)

- Jet fuel costs spike with oil prices — direct margin compression
- Gulf airspace closures disrupt Europe–Asia routing, increasing operating costs[^12]
- IAG and easyJet fell 4–6% in London on March 2[^1]; same logic applies to HVN and VJC
- HVN already in precarious financial condition; a sustained oil spike worsens its outlook

### Rubber & tire exporters (CSM, DRC)

- Synthetic rubber (petrochemical derivative) costs could rise 15–25%[^13]
- US imposed a new 10% tariff on all imports from February 24, 2026 — on top of existing anti-dumping duties of 26–46% on Asian tires[^13][^14]
- CSM's primary export market is the US — double headwind of cost + tariff
- This is the single worst-positioned sector in a prolonged conflict

### Import-heavy consumer goods / manufacturing

- Higher oil = higher transport and raw material costs
- Limited pricing power in competitive domestic market
- Inflationary pressure may reduce consumer spending

## Risk management

1. **Position sizing.** This is a geopolitical event trade — inherently binary. Size positions conservatively (no more than 5–10% of portfolio per name).
2. **Stop losses.** Set clear stop losses, particularly on oil & gas names which can reverse sharply if ceasefire is announced.
3. **Monitor daily.** Key indicators to watch: Brent crude price, Hormuz shipping traffic (Lloyd's List), urea FOB prices, VN-Index foreign flow data.
4. **Scenario trigger.** If Trump announces ceasefire or Hormuz reopens within 7 days → take profit on commodity longs immediately. If conflict enters week 3 → add to fertilizer and DGC positions as earnings revisions materialize.
5. **Diversification.** Do not concentrate entirely in war beneficiaries. Maintain core holdings in domestically-oriented, non-commodity sectors as a base.
